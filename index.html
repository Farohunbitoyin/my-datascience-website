Bank Customer Churn Analysis

In this Bank Customer Churn Analysis project, I explored a dataset of 10,000 customers across three countries to uncover patterns behind customer retention and attrition. Exploratory analysis showed that 79.6% of customers were retained while 20.4% had churned, highlighting a significant challenge for profitability. Numerical features such as Age, Balance, and Credit Score were binned and analyzed to better capture behavioral differences between retained and churned groups. To build predictive capability, I applied Decision Tree and Random Forest classifiers, using GridSearchCV and cross-validation for model optimization. A critical issue of data leakage was identified and resolved, which ensured trustworthy evaluation results. The final Random Forest model achieved strong generalizability, with 97% recall for retained customers and 47% recall for churned customers, supported by insights from feature importance analysis. Recommendations included hyperparameter tuning, feature engineering, and class imbalance handling techniques to further improve churn sensitivity and create a deployable pipeline for production.

Impact Statement: Built a churn prediction model for a bank‚Äôs 10,000 customers, uncovering key behavioral drivers of attrition and delivering actionable insights to improve customer retention strategies.

Time Series Forecasting: Daily Minimum Temperatures

In this Time Series Forecasting project, I analyzed ten years of Australia‚Äôs daily minimum temperature data to build predictive models for temperature forecasting. The analysis began with exploring the temporal structure of the data, checking for stationarity, seasonality, trends, and autocorrelation using statistical tests and visualizations. A baseline one-step prediction model was established, followed by more advanced approaches including Autoregression (AR), ARIMA, and Seasonal ARIMA (SARIMA) models. To strengthen the analysis, I also experimented with resampling techniques such as downsampling and upsampling to better understand long-term patterns. Decomposition methods, both additive and multiplicative, were applied to separate trend, seasonality, and residual components. Using a dummy dataset of U.S. airline monthly aircraft miles flown, I further demonstrated techniques such as differencing, persistence modeling, moving averages, and walk-forward validation to illustrate model robustness.

The final SARIMA model showed strong performance in capturing both trend and seasonality, making it well-suited for forecasting daily temperature values. Recommendations included hyperparameter tuning for ARIMA-family models, extending walk-forward validation for more realistic evaluation, and exploring advanced deep learning approaches such as LSTMs for future work.

YouTube NLP Sentiment Analysis

In this YouTube NLP project, I performed sentiment analysis on user comments to uncover audience perceptions and engagement patterns. Comments were preprocessed by handling missing values and applying the TextBlob library to extract polarity and subjectivity scores, categorizing each comment into positive, negative, or neutral sentiments. To make the insights more actionable, the most positive and most negative comments were highlighted with their corresponding sentiment scores. The sentiment distribution was visualized through bar and pie charts, while WordClouds were generated for both positive and negative comments to reveal dominant themes in user feedback.

In addition to comment-level analysis, engagement metrics were explored by analyzing trending tags, and a regression analysis between views and likes provided further insights into content performance. This end-to-end workflow combined natural language processing with visualization to generate meaningful insights into how viewers interact with content on YouTube.

Impact: Built an NLP pipeline to classify YouTube comments by sentiment, visualize audience feedback, and uncover engagement patterns through text and trend analysis.

Heart Attack Analysis Project

The goal of this project was to predict the likelihood of heart attacks using patient health records and to derive clinically relevant insights. Exploratory data analysis involved visualizing categorical and continuous variables, identifying and removing outliers, and addressing target imbalance. Statistical insights highlighted gender, chest pain, ECG results, and fasting blood sugar as critical features influencing heart attack risk. Multiple models were trained and compared, including Logistic Regression, Support Vector Classification, Random Forest, XGBoost, and K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN). Data preprocessing steps included scaling and transformation to ensure optimal model performance. While Support Vector Classification produced the highest accuracy, simpler models such as Logistic Regression and KNN were preferred due to their interpretability and more realistic accuracy range (72‚Äì82%). Hyperparameter tuning with GridSearchCV further optimized results.

The project provided actionable recommendations, such as targeted screening for males, sensitization about chest pain symptoms, routine home monitoring of vitals, annual ECG tests for high-risk groups, and lifestyle interventions to control fasting blood sugar.
